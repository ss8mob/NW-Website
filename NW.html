Welcome to Your
PVE Neighborhood Watch Information Site  

PVE Neighborhood Watch is a non-profit (501c3) organization founded in 1980 to encourage neighbors to watch out for one another and work with our PVE Police Department to report suspicious activities in our community.  

PVE Neighborhood Watch is all of  us, working together.

This is your location for recommendations on keeping your home and family safe, community events and more.  

If you see something, say something.  
​
Non-emergencies:  310.378.4211
Emergencies:  911  

TIP:   Make the Non-emergency number a favorite on your cell phone. Cell phone calls to 911 may be directed to an agency outside PVE. 
How You Can Get Involved  

​Become a Member:
Membership is $10 per year which supports our organization and keeps you connected as a key part of our neighborhood safety strategy.  Join us in making our community and neighborhoods safer!!   Click for more Information .  
​
Have a Block Meeting or Street Meet:
The core of Neighborhood Watch is to get to know our neighbhors and what is happening in our neighborhood. A Block Meeting or Street Meet is a simple way to start.  For more information, contact [email protected]

Become an Area Coordinator: 
A Neighborhood Watch Area Coordinator serves as the primary liaison between residents, Block Captains, law enforcement, and the Neighborhood Watch organization. The Area Coordinator helps recruit and support volunteers, shares safety information, promotes community engagement, and works to strengthen neighborhood communication and crime prevention efforts within their assigned area. Find out more on the Become Area Coordinator page.  

Become a Block Captain: 
The cornerstone of Neighborhood Watch is our Block Captains.  To be one is rewarding, easy and you'll have help along the way. Find out more on the Volunteering page.  

Participate in our Community Events
Discover a number of fun and informative events on our PVE Neighborhood Watch calendar.  They are "COVID-Friendly" and many help our local businesses.  Find out more on our Calendar page.

What's New with PVE NEIGHBORHOOD Watch --

Get Ready for the Rains
Forecasters are predicting an El Niño this year and lots of rains are sure to come.  Make sure you've prepared your home, property and emergency kit so you are ready to face the storms.   Find out more in the Get Ready for Rain   section.  
E-Bike Safety
E-Bikes are a popular way to get around the hills of Palos Verdes.  There a many types of e-bikes and special considerations for determining if a youth is able to handle one.  PVE is working to educate our residents and youth on ways to be safe, courteous and lawful. Check out resources from recent events.   Find out more in the  e-Bike Safety  section.  
Security Camera Selection Tools
Find out information about security camera selection and our PVE PD camera registration program.  Find out more in the  Security Cameras  section.  
Be Safe While Walking and Hiking 
We've got a lot of wonderful options for walking and hiking.  Make sure that you and your family are safe and aware by engaging these timely suggestions. Find out life-saving tips on our  Walking & Hiking Safety page.  
Support our Local Businesses
Discover the local businesses in our community that make it so special.  Find out more in the  Shop Local section.   ​   
See our Crime Prevention & Safety Tools and Information
If you've missed one of our blasts or want some extra information about how to help your family, home and property be more prepared against crime, disasters and emergencies, make sure you you stop by our Crime & Safety section.   ​   
Keep Your Incoming and Outgoing Mail Safe from Theft
Get tips on how to keep your mail and identity safe.  Mail theft continues to be too common an occurrence in PVE and nearby cities.  Find out more by visiting our Mailbox Safety page.  
Get Tips on Social Media Safety for You and Your Family
Crime can come through many different mediums.  Social Media can provide "bad guys" with infomation for break-ins or bullying.  Find out how you can minimize your exposure.   ​  Find out more on our  Social Media Safety  page.
